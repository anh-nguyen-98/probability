Coupon collector problem

Description: There are n coupons in a collection. A collector has the ability to purchase a coupon, but can’t choose the coupon he purchases. Instead, the coupon is revealed to be coupon i with probability pi = 1/n. Let N be the number of coupons he’ll need to collect before he has at least one coupon of each type. Let’s call this random variable N. Now, this tool helps you solve the following problems:

  • P1 The expected value of N when the coupons have equal probabilities of being collected.
  • P2 The expected value of N when the coupons have unequal probabilities of being collected.
  • P3 The variance of N when the coupons have equal probabilities of being collected.
  • P4 The variance of N when the coupons have unequal probabilities of being collected.
  • P5 The density function of N (meaning the entire distribution) when the coupons have equal probabilities.
  • P6 The density function of N (meaning the entire distribution) when the coupons have unequal probabilities.

QUEUEING THEORY PROBLEM

DESCRIPTION

A system (e.g. banks, supermarkets, computers, public transport) has to deal with problems related to queueing (waiting). The tools helps congestion analysis in the system.
A typical system involves two subjects (1) servers, (2) customers. It is always assumed that there is just a single queue (waiting line) and customers move from this single queue to the servers.
There are different types of queing systenms, whose general notation is A/S/c. Each element stands for:

  • A: Arrival process of customers.
    In reality, most arrival process follows Markov (M) model (exponential probability density).
  • S: Server process: either
    • M (Markov): Exponential probability density
    • G (General): Any arbitrary probability distribution
  • c: Number of servers
The tools helps congestion analysis in the system by answers to the following problems:
  • What is the mean customer inter-arrival rate?
  • What is the mean service rate?
  • What is the variance of inter-arrival time?
  • What is the variance of service time?
  • What is the probability that the server is busy?
  • What is the mean number of customers in the queue?
  • What is the mean waiting time in the queue?
  • What is the mean waiting time in the system
  • What is the mean number of customers in the system?
  • What is the probability that the server is idle?
